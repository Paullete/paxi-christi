Pacific Island Nations Demand Climate Justice

At COP28, Pacific Island leaders made a powerful plea for climate justice, urging wealthy nations to act before their homelands are lost to rising seas. Tuvalu’s Prime Minister Feleti Teo and Fiji’s Prime Minister Sitiveni Rabuka called for urgent measures, including phasing out fossil fuels and fulfilling climate finance commitments. Tuvalu has already begun relocation agreements, recognizing that without decisive action, entire nations could vanish.

Faith-based and civil society organizations across the region are amplifying these demands, emphasizing that climate action is not just political,it is a moral responsibility. As world leaders negotiate, the Pacific faces a fight for survival. The question remains: Will the world listen before it’s too late?
